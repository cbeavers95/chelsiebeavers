YouTube App

Solution Overview: Allow users to create, edit, and share content with one another using collaborative playlists.

Role: UX/UI Designer, UX Researcher

Timeline: January 2020 - May 2020

Background

YouTube does not allow users to directly share multiple videos and playlists with friends. When a group of people desire to send YouTube videos to one other, each person must open the app, find the option to share the video, copy the link, choose an external app, open the external app, experience a few seconds of internet lag, paste the link, send the link, return back to YouTube, and repeat the process for each video before others are able to also enjoy the viewing experience.

User Research Findings

Trends:
  • People have a hard time following all the steps to share a video.
  • People are reluctant to share a video because they are unsure of their friends' interests.
  • People have a difficult time finding new content to share with friends because the YouTube algorithm chooses suggested content based on personal history.
Insights:
  • Users want to interact with their friends.
  • Users prefer to not need to leave the YouTube app to share videos.
  • Users prefer to not need to leave the YouTube app to share videos.
  • Users want control over what they desire to see and who can see what they share.
  • Users favor the ability to keep track of their friends’ interests to share particular videos.

People Problem

YouTube allows users to find and share content that friends might also be interested in. However, the process is challenging because:

  1. People have a hard time following all the steps to share a video with friends.
  2. Users are unsure of their friends’ tastes.
  3. People have a difficult time finding new content.

Brainstorming

Approaches

Solution

Of the three initial approaches, “Must Watch” was chosen because it solves the people problem by allowing users to:

  • Share videos without additional steps beyond features that already exist within the app.
  • Look over previous suggestions by their friends to identify patterns and similar interests.
  • Watch and store new videos suggested by other friends in one place.
